Conversational Programming Experiment

Experiment Information

  • Natural language is the language we use naturally (as contrasted with an artificial language or computer code). This experiment tests the effectiveness of a natural language, voice-first system, and compares it against a natural language, text-based system and a system with both text and voice programming features.
  • The experiment consists of 1) a few short surveys, 2) several different goals you need to achieve through the three programming systems (voice only, text only, voice and text).
  • You should expect the experiment to last approximately 2 hours.
  • You will be able to see your progress at the bottom of the screen on the following pages.
  • You will be able to view examples/hints on a sidebar on the right side of the screen on the following pages.

How the Experiment Works

For each programming system, you will be provided a goal or task to achieve (for example: "Design a video game character.") You will try to do so within the constraints of the programming system by speaking or typing natural language.

After you achieve each goal, you will complete a short survey. After you have completed all goals, you will be asked to compare the three systems.
